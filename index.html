Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla life:

    1856

  • Born in Smiljan, Austro-Hungarian Empire (present-day Croatia), during a thunderstorm

    • 1875-1878

  • He studied at the Austrian Polytechnic School in Graz
  • Discover the principles of the rotating magnetic field

    • 1881

  • Works at the Central Telegraph Office in Budapest
  • Develops the idea of ​​the induction motor

    • 1882

  • Moves to Paris, works for the Continental Edison Company
  • Build the first prototype induction motor

    • 1884

  • June: Arrives in the United States with a letter of recommendation for Thomas Edison
  • Briefly works for Edison Machine Works
  • December: Resignation after disagreements with Edison over alternating current vs. direct current

    • 1885

  • Founded the Tesla Electric Light Company
  • Develops patents for electric arc lighting systems

    • 1887

  • Patents the polyphase induction motor
  • Develops the complete alternating current system

    • 1888

  • May: Presents his alternating current system at the American Institute of Electrical Engineers
  • George Westinghouse buys his patents for $1 million
  • The "War of the Currents" begins with Edison

    • 1891

  • Invents the Tesla coil
  • Conducts public demonstrations of wireless power transmission
  • Becomes a US citizen

    • 1893

  • Chicago World's Fair: Its AC system lights up the exhibit
  • Demonstrates wireless communication

    • 1895

  • His New York lab burns down, losing years of work
  • Designs the Niagara Falls hydroelectric power plant

    • 1896

  • August: The Niagara plant begins generating commercial power with its AC system

    • 1898

  • Invents the remote control
  • Demonstrates a radio-controlled boat at Madison Square Garden

    • 1899-1900

  • Establishes laboratory in Colorado Springs
  • Experiment with long-distance wireless power transmission
  • Claims to have received signals from Mars

    • 1901-1902

  • Construction begins on Wardenclyffe Tower on Long Island
  • J.P. Morgan-funded project for transatlantic communications

    • 1906

  • Demonstrates bladeless turbine
  • He patented various inventions related to energy.

    • 1915

  • Sues Marconi for infringing its radio patents
  • Growing financial problems

    • 1917

  • Wardenclyffe Tower is demolished due to debts
  • Receives the Edison Medal from the American Institute of Electrical Engineers

    • 1931

  • Appears on the cover of Time magazine for her 75th birthday
  • Announces theories about cosmic energy

    • 1934-1940

  • He lives secluded in the Hotel New Yorker
  • Continue experimenting and developing theories
  • Talks about "death rays" and directed energy weapons

    • 1943

  • January 7: He dies alone in his room at the New Yorker Hotel at the age of 86.
  • January 10: The US government classifies its documents
  • His remains are cremated and sent to Belgrade

    • Posthumous Legacy

  • 1960: The unit of magnetic flux density is named "tesla" in his honor
  • 1975: His ashes are transferred to the Nikola Tesla Museum in Belgrade
  • His inventions remain fundamental to modern technology.
